2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer, smartphone, tablet, or other device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, to enhance user experience, and to provide information to the site owners. Cookies are stored by your browser and contain information that can be read by the website that placed them.

Cookies serve various purposes, including:

  • Essential Functionality: Enabling core features such as page navigation and access to secure areas of the website.
  • Performance: Collecting information about how visitors use the website to improve its performance and functionality.
  • Preferences: Remembering your choices and preferences to provide a more personalized experience.
  • Analytics: Helping us understand how visitors interact with our website so we can improve it.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

We use the following categories of cookies on our Website:

7. Managing Cookies Through Your Browser

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can configure your browser to:

  • Block All Cookies: Prevent websites from placing cookies on your device.
  • Delete Existing Cookies: Remove cookies that have already been stored on your device.
  • Block Third-Party Cookies: Accept only first-party cookies and block cookies from third parties.
  • Require Consent Before Setting Cookies: Configure your browser to request your permission before any website can place a cookie on your device.

The method for managing cookies varies by browser. Below are links to instructions for managing cookie settings in common browsers:

Please note that if you choose to block or delete cookies, some features of our Website may not function correctly, and your user experience may be affected.

8. Duration of Cookies

Cookies have different durations depending on their purpose:

  • Session Cookies: These cookies are temporary and are deleted automatically when you close your browser. They are used to maintain session state and enable essential functionality during your visit.
  • Persistent Cookies: These cookies remain on your device for a specified period or until you manually delete them. They are used to remember your preferences and enhance your user experience across multiple visits.
